The Core Concept:
Culture as an Organism

The Phenomenology of Culture-Systems presents civilization not as a static object, but as a dynamic, nonlinear living system. A "High Culture" is defined as a self-organizing network where human beings, artifacts, and expressions are interdependent and constantly exchanging energy and matter.

The Methodology:
Noӧdynamics

The text introduces Noӧdynamics, a scientific approach that combines phenomenology and cognitive science. Although cultural experiences are subjective, this method quantifies them to build a logical, consistent model. It aims to analyze "intentional encultured experience" to understand how human cognition created culture-systems.

The Evolutionary
Lifecycle

Culture-systems are treated as organic units with unique identities. Like biological organisms, they follow a predictable trajectory:

  • Emergence: Born from the integration of human and environmental resources.
  • Growth: Development through observable states.
  • Senescence & Death: Eventual decline and decomposition, dissipating resources back into the environment.

The Scientific
Implication

Current science lacks the language to describe these complex cultural patterns, necessitating a new 'calculus' to analyze them. The text argues that cultural forms (structure) imply an underlying evolutionary force (process), revealing hidden aspects of human nature. Noӧdynamics aims to provide these missing definitions, representing a major paradigm shift for academia and philosophy by rigorously analyzing the birth, development, and death of civilizations.
