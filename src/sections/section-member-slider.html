three primary kinds of Academics

1 / 6

Researcher

{{{picture "/images/slide-img-12.png" alt="Description" class="main-slider__slide-img"}}}

Gathers, organizes, and sometimes discovers original data for defined purposes across scientific, spiritual, artistic, philosophical, religious, and technical domains, contributing to the broader body of cultural knowledge.

Writer

{{{picture "/images/slide-img-13.png" alt="Description" class="main-slider__slide-img"}}}

Translates data into new forms or accessible texts, working with original material or research findings across scientific, cultural, institutional, and public domains, shaping understanding through communication.

Activist

{{{picture "/images/slide-img-14.png" alt="Description" class="main-slider__slide-img"}}}

Represents the Institute publicly, directing how research and writing are presented to external audiences across academic, cultural, media, and social domains, guiding outreach and public engagement.
