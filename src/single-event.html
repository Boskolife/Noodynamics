Humanlight Celebration

Palace, Floor T2/T1, Theater 2 january 26, 2026

HumanLight is a Humanist holiday celebrated annually since 2011, created to illuminate the shared values that unite humanity beyond religious or cultural boundaries. Rooted in secular humanism, HumanLight emphasizes reason, compassion, humanity, and hope—principles that guide ethical living and collective progress. This exhibition explores HumanLight not only as a holiday, but as a living philosophy expressed through art, ideas, and human connection. The exhibition invites visitors to reflect on how reason empowers us to understand the world through critical thinking, science, and evidence, encouraging informed choices and thoughtful dialogue. Through visual works, interactive installations, and narrative displays, the theme of reason is presented as a tool for liberation—allowing individuals to question, learn, and grow while respecting diverse perspectives. Compassion lies at the heart of HumanLight, and this exhibition highlights acts of empathy, solidarity, and care that define our shared humanity. Featured works examine kindness in everyday life, social responsibility, and the moral impulse to reduce suffering and promote well-being. These pieces remind us that compassion is not passive, but an active commitment to justice, inclusion, and mutual respect. Humanity, as celebrated by HumanLight, recognizes the inherent dignity and worth of every person. The exhibition showcases stories and representations of human resilience, creativity, and cooperation across cultures and generations. By centering human experience, the exhibition underscores our interconnectedness and our collective responsibility to one another. Finally, hope is presented as a forward-looking force—an optimism grounded in action rather than wishful thinking. Through hopeful visions of the future, the exhibition encourages visitors to imagine a world shaped by reasoned choices and compassionate values. HumanLight is a reminder that meaning, ethics, and celebration can be rooted in our shared human experience. This exhibition invites all visitors to engage with these values and to carry the light of humanity forward into their communities and lives.
